House Shade Simulation

This is a simulation of the shade on a house.  It computes the length of the shadows cast by an overhang and a wingwall as a function of time, date, and latitude.
  • To the left is a view of the front of the house (viewing the wingwall edge-on) and to the right is a side view.
  • Click on the front view to reposition the windows.
  • Click on the right of the side view to redefine the width of the overhang, and on the left of the side view to redefine the width of the wingwall.
  • Click on the compass to define geographic north.  The front view is facing the direction on the bottom of the compass.  For example, it starts out facing south.
  • You can type in the latitude of the house below the compass.  (Longitude is unnecessary, since we use local solar time instead of GMT.)
  • Click on the "calendar" or the clock to define a month and time.
You can run the simulation by clicking on the "Run" button.  By checking "Run year" you can cycle through a day at a second per hour; by checking"Run day" you can run through the year at a second per month.
